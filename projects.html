AUDREY GEEHAN

Chameleon: Chameleons are distinctive reptiles known for their ability to change color, which they use for camouflage, communication, and temperature regulation. They are found primarily in Africa, Madagascar, and southern Asia, and they have specialized features such as independently mobile eyes and long, sticky tongues used to catch prey.

Cobra: Cobras are venomous snakes found primarily in Africa and Asia. They are known for their iconic hood, which they spread when threatened to appear larger and more intimidating. Cobras are highly venomous and can deliver potent neurotoxins through their fangs, making them both feared and respected in many cultures.

Bearded Dragon: Bearded dragons are medium-sized lizards native to Australia. They are popular reptile pets known for their docile nature and distinctive spiny "beard" under their chin, which they can puff out when threatened or to display dominance.

Gecko: Geckos are small to medium-sized lizards found in various parts of the world, including tropical and subtropical regions. They are known for their adhesive toe pads that allow them to climb smooth surfaces, and many species are popular as pets due to their unique appearance and relatively low maintenance.

Tree Frog: Tree frogs are small, arboreal amphibians found in various parts of the world, including tropical rainforests and temperate forests. They are named for their ability to climb and live in trees, and they have specialized toe pads that allow them to adhere to surfaces. Tree frogs are known for their vibrant colors and often have a unique croaking call used for communication and mating rituals.
